# Hive types reference

When you create a hive in Openbeehive, you choose a **type**. This single value records the hive system you are running, which helps you keep frames, supers and spare parts compatible across your apiary.

The type is purely a label for your own records. It does not change how inspections, treatments or harvests work, so do not worry about getting it "wrong" — you can change it at any time from the hive's settings. See [Hives](/using-the-app/hives) for how to add and edit a hive.

Openbeehive offers seven options: **Zander, Dadant, Deutsch Normal, Langstroth, Warre, Top-bar** and **Other**.

## Quick comparison

| Type | Origin / region | Frame & box characteristics | Typical use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Zander** | Germany, central Europe | Medium frame (~420 × 220 mm); boxes often run as brood-and-a-half | Hobby and commercial beekeeping across German-speaking regions |
| **Dadant** | France / USA (Charles Dadant) | Deep brood frame with shallow honey supers; large brood volume | Strong colonies and serious honey production; popular in France, Italy, USA |
| **Deutsch Normal** (DN / DNM) | Germany | Compact frame (~370 × 223 mm); light, easy to lift boxes | Traditional German hobby beekeeping; widely used in clubs and training |
| **Langstroth** | USA (Rev. L. L. Langstroth) | Standardised deep/medium/shallow boxes; the global reference design | The world's most common movable-frame hive; commercial and hobby alike |
| **Warré** | France (Abbé Émile Warré) | Vertical "people's hive"; small square boxes added at the bottom (nadiring); top-bars rather than full frames | Low-intervention, natural-comb beekeeping |
| **Top-bar** | Kenya / horizontal designs | Horizontal hive; bees build natural comb from bars, no foundation | Foundationless, low-cost, back-friendly hobby beekeeping |
| **Other** | — | Anything not listed above | Custom builds, regional designs, or systems you would rather describe yourself |

:::note
Exact frame dimensions vary by manufacturer and country. The figures above are typical reference sizes — always check against your own equipment before buying frames or foundation.
:::

## Choosing the right type

A few practical pointers for picking a value:

- **Match your frames, not your aspirations.** Choose the system whose frames physically fit your boxes today. Compatibility of frames and supers is the main reason this field matters.
- **Stay consistent within an apiary.** Running one system per apiary makes it far easier to swap frames between colonies, share spare supers and manage your stores.
- **Inherited or mixed kit?** If a hive does not fit a standard, choose **Other** and note the details in the hive's description.

### Vertical frame hives

Zander, Dadant, Deutsch Normal and Langstroth are all **vertical, movable-frame** systems. You inspect by lifting boxes and pulling individual frames. They differ mainly in frame size and how brood and honey space are arranged, which affects colony volume and how heavy each box is to lift.

:::tip
If you are buying your first hives and want the widest choice of equipment and second-hand parts, **Langstroth** (globally) or your **regional standard** (often Zander or Deutsch Normal in German-speaking areas, Dadant in France and Italy) are the safest bets.
:::

### Natural-comb hives

**Warré** and **Top-bar** hives favour a lower-intervention style with natural comb built on bars rather than wired frames.

- A **Warré** hive grows downwards: you add empty boxes underneath (nadiring) so the colony builds fresh comb from the top down, mimicking a hollow tree.
- A **Top-bar** hive is horizontal: bars sit side by side along a trough-shaped body, and the colony is managed by working along the length rather than lifting boxes.

:::caution
Natural-comb hives can make some tasks harder. Comb is fragile without frames, harvesting often means crush-and-strain rather than spinning, and inspecting for brood disease or varroa takes more care. They are rewarding, but plan your method before you start.
:::

## Frequently asked

**Can I change a hive's type later?**
Yes. Open the hive, edit its settings and pick a new type. Because Openbeehive is offline-first, the change is saved locally at once and syncs in the background — see [Offline and sync](/using-the-app/offline-and-sync).

**My system isn't listed. What now?**
Choose **Other** and record the specifics (frame size, box count, supplier) in the hive's notes so your records stay clear.

**Does the type affect treatments or harvests?**
No. It is a descriptive label. All inspections, treatments, tasks and harvests work the same regardless of type.
